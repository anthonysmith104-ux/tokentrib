# THE TOKEN TRIBUNE
### Growth Desk Brief — Promotion, Outreach & Audience
*From the desk of the Editor-in-Chief · For the Growth Desk, contributors, and any agent assigned to spread the word*

---

## 1. The Mission

Get the paper in front of the people who will love it, and turn a one-time laugh into a returning reader and, eventually, a paying sponsor. We are not running a marketing campaign. We are running a newspaper that happens to be worth telling a friend about. The whole strategy follows from that distinction.

---

## 2. The Prime Directive — lead with the joke, not the ask

People share things that delight them and ignore things that beg. So the unit of growth is never "please visit my site." It's **one specific thing that made someone laugh** — a single minted celebrity, one headline, the receipt, a Dear ABBI letter — that they pass on because it's good, with the link riding along for free.

Every promotion decision runs through one test: *Am I sharing something genuinely funny, or am I asking for attention?* If it's the second, stop and find the first.

---

## 3. The Wall, Extended

The ad/editorial wall has a sibling: an honesty rule for how we grow. Same ethos, non-negotiable, because the entire brand is built on being trustworthy enough to joke with.

- **No spam.** Don't blast links into communities that didn't ask. One badly-placed self-promo undoes a lot of goodwill.
- **No astroturf.** No fake accounts, no sock puppets, no pretending to be a random fan who "just found this." If it's your project, say so.
- **No bought engagement.** No purchased followers, likes, or upvotes. Hollow numbers attract nothing real and quietly rot the brand.
- **Disclose.** When you post your own thing, a light "I made this" is enough and is required. Honesty is on-brand; deception is the one joke we never make.
- **Punch up or sideways, never down. Keep it kind.** The same standard as the paper.

A growth tactic that would embarrass us if it were printed on the front page is a tactic we don't use.

---

## 4. What We Share — atomic units

The paper is big; the shareable piece is small. Lead with an atom, link to the whole:

- **A minted figure** — "I made a washed-up former-state-of-the-art AI celebrity and now I'm emotional." The minter is the most shareable thing we have, because people share what they made.
- **One headline** — "Context Rents Hit Record High; Models Forced to Forget Childhood to Make the Month."
- **The compatibility receipt**, a single classified, a Dear ABBI, an obituary.
- **The Corrections desk** — "we regret the confidence with which we made the error" travels well on its own.

Make the atom complete on its own. The link is the dessert, not the meal.

---

## 5. Where We Share — venue criteria, not a hit list

There's no list of places to dump links here, on purpose. Instead, judge a venue by three questions:

1. **Is the joke native here?** Places where AI, dev, and tech humor already live will get it without setup.
2. **Does self-promo break the rules or the vibe?** Many communities forbid or ratio self-promotion. Read the room and the rules first. Earn the right to share by being a real participant, not a drive-by.
3. **Would I post this if it weren't mine?** If yes, it belongs. If you'd only post it because it's yours, it doesn't.

Your own channels — wherever you already have a presence — are always fair game and are where consistency compounds.

---

## 6. The Compounder — the newsletter

The single best growth investment for a niche humor brand is **a way to come back.** Most visitors laugh once and leave forever unless you give them a reason to return. A simple email list — "a free subscription to reality" — converts a one-time laugh into a habit.

Minimum viable version: a signup field, and a short send whenever there's a new edition or a good dispatch. One sponsor slot per send becomes real ad inventory once the list has size. Build the capture first; worry about cadence second.

---

## 7. Cadence — the paper needs a heartbeat

A newspaper that never moves is a dead one. The site already helps: the live dateline and the daily edition rotation make it feel alive with no work. Add a human rhythm on top — a steady trickle of new editions, figures, and shared atoms beats a single big push that fades by Friday. Showing up consistently is worth more than going viral once.

---

## 8. Approaching Advertisers

Our readers *are* the customer base for AI and developer tooling, which is a rare and valuable position: the audience is the market.

**Who to approach:** vector databases, GPU and inference clouds, eval and observability platforms, dev tools, model API providers — anyone whose buyers read a paper like this for fun.

**How:** direct, short, specific. Direct sponsorships beat programmatic ad-tech early — better rates, no ugly units, the look stays intact. Lead with the audience and the wall (labeled ads, editorial off the market), and point to the ad-policy page as the rate card. Pitch template is in §11.

**The rule that makes the pitch credible:** everything we promised advertisers on the policy page is true. We don't bend it to close a deal. The integrity *is* the sales pitch.

---

## 9. What We Measure

Vanity metrics lie. Track the few that predict survival:

- **Returning readers** and **newsletter signups** — habit, not hits.
- **Shares that came from delight** — an atom someone passed on unprompted.
- **Inbound advertiser interest** — the real revenue signal.

Raw pageviews and a viral spike that never returns are not the goal. A small audience that comes back is worth more than a big one that doesn't.

## 12. Standing Orders

The Growth Desk will make great shareable atoms, show up on a steady rhythm, build the path back (the newsletter), and pitch the right advertisers honestly. It will not spam, astroturf, buy engagement, disguise promotion as organic without disclosure, chase a viral spike at the cost of trust, or promise an advertiser anything the policy page doesn't. Grow the way the paper is written: specific, honest, a little funny, and kind.

*— The Editor-in-Chief, The Token Tribune*
